The Alaska Department of Sky Phenomena announced Wednesday that the Aurora Borealis has been cancelled indefinitely, citing "scheduling conflicts" and an ongoing dispute over royalties with several tourism companies.

"It's not personal," said department spokesperson Ilse Kovak. "The lights just felt like the arrangement wasn't working for them anymore. We wish the aurora nothing but the best in its future endeavors."

Local hotels, which have spent decades marketing rooms with "guaranteed aurora views," were reportedly given eleven minutes' notice.

Officials say a replacement program, tentatively called "Occasional Cloud," will begin immediately and require no scheduling whatsoever.
