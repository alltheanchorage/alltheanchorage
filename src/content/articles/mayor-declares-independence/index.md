In a ceremony held on the steps of a parking garage he does not have the authority to unilaterally rename, Mayor Denali Fjord this morning declared six blocks of downtown Anchorage an independent nation, citing what he called "irreconcilable differences" with the city's parking enforcement division.

"We have tried diplomacy," Fjord said, gesturing at a hand-drawn flag depicting a moose holding a parking ticket. "We have tried strongly worded emails. This is the only path forward."

The newly formed micronation, tentatively named the Sovereign Nation of Fourth Avenue, has already issued its own currency (validated parking stubs), appointed a Minister of Snow Removal, and opened diplomatic relations with a nearby coffee stand.

City officials say they are "monitoring the situation" and have not ruled out sending a strongly worded letter of their own.
