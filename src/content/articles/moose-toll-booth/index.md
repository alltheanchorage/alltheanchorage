The Anchorage City Council voted 6-1 Tuesday to install a toll booth at the intersection of Tudor Road and O'Malley, citing a troubling rise in moose who "clearly know what a crosswalk is now."

"We used to be able to tell them apart from regular traffic," said Councilmember Ruth Petrov. "Now they're checking for cars, waiting for the light, sometimes even making eye contact with drivers. Frankly, it's showing us up."

Under the new ordinance, moose will be charged $2.50 per crossing, payable in willow branches or exact change. A moose caught jaywalking will be let off with a warning, since officials admit "that's actually more on-brand."

The toll booth is expected to be operational by spring, pending the hiring of an attendant willing to work within antler range.
